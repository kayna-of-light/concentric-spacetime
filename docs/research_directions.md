# Research Directions: The Concentric System on S² × R⁺

## What We Have

A manifold (S² × R⁺) where the four primes emerge from geometry, not from postulation. The mathematics is structurally identical to the hydrogen atom — not by analogy but by identity. This means we inherit a century of refined mathematical tools.

---

## 1. Spectroscopy of Spiritual States

The hydrogen atom is understood through its *spectrum* — the frequencies of light it emits when transitioning between shells. We have the same structure: discrete energy levels $E_n = -1/(2n^2)$, transition energies $\Delta E = E_n - E_m$, and selection rules governing which transitions are allowed ($\Delta l = \pm 1$).

**The work:** Compute the complete transition spectrum of the concentric system — every allowed jump between states, its energy, its angular momentum change.

**What it means:** The mathematical equivalent of asking: *what are the allowed transformations between spiritual states, and what does each one cost or release?*

---

## 2. Selection Rules as Spiritual Law

In hydrogen, not every transition is allowed. The selection rules ($\Delta l = \pm 1$, $\Delta m = 0, \pm 1$) come from angular momentum conservation — from the geometry of the sphere.

**The work:** Map the selection rules to the correspondential framework. A soul at $l=2$ can move to $l=1$ or $l=3$, but not directly to $l=0$. The vertical degree structure imposes constraints on which jumps are possible.

**What it means:** Testable against the regeneration literature — does Swedenborg's account of spiritual progression obey these selection rules? Can a soul skip degrees, or must it traverse them one at a time?

## 3. Perturbation Theory — Deformation of the Sphere

The perfectly spherical system is the idealized case. Real atoms sit in external fields (Stark effect, Zeeman effect) that break symmetry and split degenerate energy levels.

**The work:** Apply perturbation theory to ask: *what happens to the concentric system when external influence (influx, temptation, ruling love) deforms the sphere?* The Stark effect (electric field → linear perturbation) and Zeeman effect (magnetic field → angular perturbation) have direct correspondential analogues.

**What it means:** A rigorous framework for studying how spiritual states respond to perturbation — how influx lifts degeneracies, how temptation breaks symmetry.

## 4. The Flatness Limit — A Quantitative Theory of the Proprium

We showed that as $R \to \infty$ (sphere flattens), eigenvalue gaps $\to 0$ and all states become indistinguishable.

**The work:** Compute the rate of degeneration as a function of $R$. How quickly does structure dissolve? Is it gradual or is there a critical radius below which structure is robust and above which it collapses? Characterize the phase transition (if one exists) — the mathematical boundary between a self with real interior structure and the proprium's flatland.

**What it means:** The proprium is not absence of self but *flattened* self — a geometry where the vertical degrees have collapsed and nothing is distinguishable from anything else. This direction gives a quantitative measure of how far from centre a configuration has drifted.

## 6. Multi-Particle Extension — Societies of Souls

Hydrogen is one electron. Helium is two. The periodic table is what happens when you add electrons to the same potential and apply the Pauli exclusion principle. We have the same shell structure.

**The work:** Treat distinct configurations as identical fermions filling shells. Derive the classification of collective states by their shell-filling configuration — the **periodic table of spiritual societies**.

**What it means:** Connects directly to Swedenborg's description of heavenly societies as organized by ruling love and degree. The Pauli exclusion principle (no two fermions in the same state) has a correspondential reading: no two souls occupy the identical spiritual configuration — each is unique within the shared structure.

## 7. Entanglement — The Influx Mechanism Formalized

In quantum mechanics, particles sharing the same potential become entangled — measuring one constrains the state of the other. The correlation is not transmitted through space; it is imposed by the shared geometry.

**The work:** If two configurations share the same S² × R⁺ manifold, their states are correlated by the geometry itself. Formalize this as a model of influx: not a mysterious "flow" from one being to another, but a geometric correlation imposed by sharing the same curved space.

**What it means:** Influx is not a substance that travels. It is the consequence of two configurations existing on the same manifold — their states are already correlated because the geometry that defines them is shared. Distance between them is not spatial but state-difference (NB08). Entanglement is what "being on the same manifold" looks like from inside.
