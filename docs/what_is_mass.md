## What Mass Is

### Mass is resistance to alignment

Charge, spin, color, chirality — these are discrete labels from the CRT decomposition of $\mathbb{Z}^*_{210}$: $a_3$ = chirality, $a_5$ = charge sector, $a_7$ = generation × color-parity. These tell you **which mode** you're looking at. They're integers. They don't need dynamics.

Mass is different. Mass is the continuous quantity that distinguishes an electron from a muon — particles that interact identically with every force except gravity and inertia. Mass measures **how stubbornly a mode resists alignment with the center.**

The CP ratio measures exactly this. Take two CRT sectors that define a conjugate pair. The cascade runs identically for both — same ODE, same κ, same ω. But at the coprime crossings, the two sectors have different residual amplitudes. One wraps (its residual exceeds ±π, meaning it has passed beyond the range where direct correction can reach), the other doesn't. The ratio of their RMS residuals is the CP ratio.

A heavier particle's mode is more deeply trapped in misalignment — the covering potential pulls on it, but the wrapping holds it. A lighter particle's mode relaxes more toward the center. Mass is the measurable trace of this differential resistance.

### Why the exponential and the character count

The mass formula is $m_{\text{heavy}}/m_{\text{light}} = \text{CP}^{N/(2\pi)}$ where $N$ is the number of Fourier characters visible at that level of the covering tower.

Each character is an independent mode of misalignment. They form an orthogonal basis — they don't interfere. The total misalignment of a sector is the **product** of per-character contributions, because independent contributions multiply. Product → exponential: $N$ characters each contributing factor $f$ gives $f^N$. The CP ratio is $f$. The character count is $N$.

Why $1/(2\pi)$? Because each character completes one full $2\pi$-cycle per primorial window. The effective exponent is the character count normalized by the cycle length.

NB133 established this: the exponent is character counting, not dynamics. The dissipation eigenvalue $\gamma_3 = p_4^2 = 49$ equals the character count + 1 only because of the four-prime cooperation identity $\prod_{k=1}^{3}(p_k - 1) = p_4 + 1$, which is specific to {2,3,5,7}. The bridge between dissipation and mass exponents is arithmetic — a property of the primes — not a causal link through the dynamics.

### Why mass and not something else

The solenoid cascade produces many quantities: sector RMS values, filter gains, Fourier coefficients, transient amplitudes, steady-state profiles. Three filters select which quantities correspond to physical observables:

1. **Gauge invariance**: Individual sector RMS values depend on coordinate choices. CP *ratios* don't — coordinate-dependent factors cancel. Only gauge-invariant quantities are observable.

2. **Convergence**: Most cascade quantities depend on integration time $T$. CP ratios converge to T-independent values (NB97, NB106). Measurable quantities don't depend on how long you observe.

3. **Discriminant power**: Many converged, gauge-invariant quantities are universal — the same for all fermion types. The filter gain |H₃|, the eigenvalues, the total energy. These describe properties of the vacuum, not of particles. CP ratios **differ per CRT label** — they distinguish one fermion from another.

Mass ratios are the only quantities that pass all three filters simultaneously. That is why they are real. Everything else either depends on coordinates, depends on observation time, or cannot tell one fermion from another.
